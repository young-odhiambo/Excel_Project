# Jumia Product Performance Dashboard

## Project Overview

This project analyzes product performance data from Jumia using Microsoft Excel. 
The objective was to identify relationships between discounts, ratings, reviews, and product pricing while building an interactive dashboard for business decision-making.

The project demonstrates:
- Data cleaning
- Excel formulas
- PivotTables
- PivotCharts
- Slicers and KPI cards
- Dashboard design


## Project Objectives

The analysis aimed to answer the following business questions:

- Does offering higher discounts lead to more customer reviews?
- Do highly rated products have higher prices?
- Which products receive the highest number of reviews?
- Which products have the highest discounts?
- What is the distribution of product ratings?
- What is the distribution of discount categories?
- Which products have the highest and lowest ratings?
- What are the most and least expensive products?

## Analysis Performed

### 1. Relationship Between Discount Percentage and Reviews

A scatter plot was created to determine whether products with higher discounts receive more customer reviews.

**Finding**

A weak negative relationship exists between discount percentage and customer reviews. Higher discounts do not necessarily generate more customer engagement.


### 2. Relationship Between Ratings and Reviews

The relationship between product ratings and customer reviews was analyzed.

**Finding**

There is little to no correlation between product ratings and the number of reviews.


### 3. Do Higher Discounts Lead to More Sales?

Customer reviews were used as an indicator of product popularity.

**Finding**

Higher discounts alone do not appear to significantly increase customer interest or sales.


### 4. Do Higher-Rated Products Have Higher Prices?

Ratings were compared against product prices.

**Finding**

Highly rated products are not always expensive. Many affordable products also receive excellent ratings.

## Business Insights

- High discounts do not guarantee increased customer engagement.
- Customer ratings have little influence on review volume.
- Affordable products can still achieve excellent ratings.
- Product quality and customer satisfaction may influence purchasing decisions more than discount levels.
- Interactive filtering allows quick comparison across rating and discount categories.
